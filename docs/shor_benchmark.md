## How to read

* **Wires** — distinct `WireID`s allocated (total, not live HWM).
  Live HWM is typically ~half: WireIDs are monotonic in `TracingContext`,
  so deallocated wires still count. For live HWM see `test/bench_shor_scaling.jl`.
* **CCX** — Toffoli count (CX with `ncontrols ≥ 1`, i.e. ≥ 3 total wires).
  Multi-controlled rotations are counted as their constituent `Ry`/`Rz`
  with `ncontrols=2`, not as CCX — see `src/channel/dag.jl`.
* **Depth** — ASAP-scheduled column count from
  `_draw_schedule_compact`. This is the pixel-renderer depth, not a
  critical-path count — serialised operations on the same wire stack
  in the same column under the compact scheduler.
* **DAG bytes** — `length(dag) × 25` per Session 3 HotNode union storage.
  Matches `sizeof(ctx.dag)` for the Vector element payload.

## Takeaways

* **Impl A (oracle lift) is the lean favourite:** 148 gates, 18 wires,
  depth 137. `oracle_table(k -> powermod(a, k, N), k_reg, Val(L))` lowers
  to a single Babbush-Gidney QROM — no mulmod cascade. Cost is
  exponential in `t` (QROM table size); fine at `t=3`, infeasible at
  `t ≥ 20`.
* **Impl B (phase-estimation HOF) is the heaviest:** 3609 gates, **217**
  wires. The `phase_estimate` HOF unrolls `2^t − 1 = 7` mulmod calls and
  each one carries a 2^(L+1)-entry QROM inside. The DAG's 217 wires are
  dominated by QROM control ancillae — Babbush-Gidney log-depth tree.
  Idiomatic but QROM-heavy.
* **Impl C (c-U^{2^j} cascade) is bimodal:** 3097 gates, 109 wires,
  depth 2702. Fewer mulmod calls than B (t vs. 2^t − 1) but each call is
  heavier — forward+inverse QROM per call, plus a half-swap ladder. The
  N=15 numbers here under-sell the L scaling; at L=6 impl C hits
  `~47M gates` (see `test/bench_shor_scaling.jl`).
* **Impl D (Beauregard arithmetic mulmod) at 19 wires, 2385 gates:**
  Polynomial in L. Wires are constant-ish across L (here 19 for L=4,
  scaling as 2L+3+t under the uf4 cascade). Gate breakdown shows the
  arithmetic signature — heavy `Rz` (1492) from QFT-adder rotations,
  light CCX (24 from when-nested modadd cascades).
* **Impl D-semi (semi-classical iQFT, Beauregard Fig 8) is the
  best-in-class:** same gate count (2373 vs 2385), same depth (1264),
  *one* fewer wire allocation (counter reused). At large `t` this
  becomes **t**-many fewer wires, independent of `L`. This is the
  polynomial-in-L / "2n+3 qubits" Beauregard record.
